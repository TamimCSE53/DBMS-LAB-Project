Introduction to Graph Theory

Graph theory is the branch of mathematics that deals with problems related to networks of points connected by lines. In computer science, it’s used to model networks, from road maps to internet routing, dependency management, and even social networks.

Why Learn Graph Theory?

Graphs are everywhere! They are used to represent and solve real-world problems such as shortest paths in navigation systems, dependencies in task scheduling, and recommendations on social platforms. Mastering graph theory gives you a competitive edge in both academic and industrial applications of computing.

What You'll Learn

  • Graph Representations (Adjacency List, Matrix)
  • Breadth First Search (BFS)
  • Depth First Search (DFS)
  • Connected Components
  • Cycle Detection (Directed and Undirected)
  • Dijkstra's and Bellman-Ford Algorithms
  • Topological Sorting
  • Bipartite Graphs
  • Bridges and Articulation Points
